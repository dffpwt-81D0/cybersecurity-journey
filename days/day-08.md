## 📘 Module 3: Protecting Your Data and Privacy

### Core Insight
Most successful attacks do not defeat sophisticated defences.
They exploit poor habits, weak configurations, and 
misplaced trust. Module 3 addresses the human and 
configuration layer of cybersecurity defence.

---

### 🔑 Password Security — Passphrases vs Passwords

| Factor | Password | Passphrase |
|--------|----------|------------|
| **Length** | Typically short | Significantly longer |
| **Complexity** | Special chars, numbers | Natural words combined |
| **Brute Force Resistance** | Lower | Much higher |
| **Memorability** | Difficult | Easier |
| **Example** | `P@ssw0rd!` | `correct-horse-battery-stable` |

> Length defeats brute force more reliably than complexity.
> A passphrase is both more secure and more memorable —
> yet most users still rely on short, complex passwords.

---

### 📡 Public Wi-Fi — Risk Surface

Public Wi-Fi networks are uncontrolled environments where:
- Traffic is visible to any device on the same network
- Man-in-the-Middle (MitM) attacks are trivially executed
- Sensitive sessions (banking, email, credentials) are 
  exposed without a VPN or encrypted connection

**The threat is not the network — it is the assumption 
that the network is safe.**

---

### 🧱 Defence in Depth

Security is not a single tool or a single decision.
It is a series of deliberate, layered controls where 
each layer compensates for the weakness of another.

Layer 1 → Strong authentication (passphrases, MFA)
Layer 2 → Network controls (firewall, VPN)
Layer 3 → Device security (antivirus, updates)
Layer 4 → User behaviour (awareness, habits)
Layer 5 → Monitoring & response (SIEM, logging)

> Remove one layer and the others become easier to bypass.
> Defence in depth is a principle, not a suggestion.
